
biotech is a massive industry. As of April 2026, the US biotech industry is valued between 550-700B with growth to between 1.25-2T by 2030-2035 at a rate of ~12-14%. 

In order to optimally participate in this space and grow to be a leader in the space, I want to learn more about all the offerings of the industry. This includes applications like surgery with all its subfields (robotic assisted Surgery (RAS), Navigation/image integration, instrumentation, surgical aims/methodology, etc.). Lists like this can be made for other sectors like implantable, regenerative medicine, biomaterials, cell engineering, therapeutics, pharmaceuticals, diagnostics, digital-health/bioinformatics, etc.

This folder will be for organizing my thoughts on these different topics. Currently, I believe a nice way to organize this is going to be listing all of these different biotech spaces, going over advances in each of the spaces, and then having some dedicated "coupled ideas" space to talk about how they play off each other. For example, evaluating how a suboptimal solution in one domain that is common in clinical practice could be better suited to a solution from another domain. Conversely, how advances in two distinct domains can be combined synergistically to create a system with substantially more utility (like imaging + surgery or digital-health + ML).
