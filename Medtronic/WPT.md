A self resonant, two sided copper foil wireless power coil:

In wireless power transfer, you aim to create resonant power and receiver coils that couple to each other through a magnetic field, thereby enabling wireless power transfer. Typical designs utilize dedicated lumped capacitor and inductor elements to achieve resonance. However, this leads to high voltage nodes in the device which can be a major hazard. More recent designs have started to explore the use of distributed capacitor systems. However, all of the existing distributed capacitance designs come with some drawback which severely limit practical use for wireless charging of human implanted devices. Specifically, they have non-uniform heating profiles which can present a danger to patients and/or limit charging rates to lower powers or they are parallel LC resonances which require a high driving voltage, which is both expensive to produce and also presents the hazard of high voltage. 

We have developed a new self-resonant structure that utilizes a series LC resonance that does not suffer from the non-uniform heating problem and is easy to manufacture. Next generation wireless chargers for patients will aim to use higher frequencies, specifically the 6.78MHz band. In this band, coil design has a much stronger impact on performance as compared to traditional, lower frequency bands. This proposed coil design minimizes eddy currents, thereby enabling higher power limits and also fits nicely into a simple and robust manufacturing processes to create a final patient charger product.
